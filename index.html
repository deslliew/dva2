Insights of Carbon Dioxide (CO2) Emissions in Asia
(2017 - 2021)


High carbon emissions have significant consequences, affecting both the environment and everyone within it. Therefore, it is imperative to closely examine these emissions for the betterment of our environment and the well-being of future generations.
In this webpage, we will be looking at different visualizations that aim to provide insights on the CO2 Emissions in Asia from 2017 to 2021.

The data used in this visualization is obtained from:
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/thedevastator/global-fossil-co2-emissions-by-country-2002-2022 and
https://population.un.org/wpp/Download/Standard/CSV/

Choropleth Map Showing the CO2 Emission Rates in Asia

This choropleth map visualizes the severity of the CO2 Emissions in Asia from 2012 to 2014. The severity of the CO2 Emission rates are shown using the blue colour luminance. The darker the shade of blue , the higher the CO2 Emission rate in the particular country, and vice versa. From the choropleth map, we know that UAE, Saudi Arabia, Mongolia and Korea are the top 5 countries with the highest CO2 Emission rates in Asia from 2017 to 2021.



Line Chart Showing the Trend of the Median CO2 Emission Rates in Asia

This line chart shows the trend of the median CO2 Emission rates (per capita) in Asia from 2017 to 2021. The median CO2 Emission rates (per capita) in Asia is around 7, but was gradually increased during the 3 years (2017 - 2019), which is visualized in the line chart, there is a slight decrease in 2020 due to the pandemic lockdown. When the world recovered from the pandemic, it started to increase again.



Donut Chart Showing the Percentage Distribution of the CO2 Emission Rates in Asia, Based on Materials

There are 5 materials, that are not part of these materials are classified as "Others":
Coal , Oil , Gas
Cement , Flaring , Others

Looking at the donut chart, we know that the Coal has the highest CO2 Emission rate among the 6 materials categories.
On the other hand, Flaring has the lowest CO2 Emission rate among the 6 materials categories.
This pattern persisted throughout the 5 years, from 2017 to 2021.



Stacked Bar Chart Showing the Difference Between the Average Crude Suicide Rates in Asia, Based on Gender

Looking at the stacked bar chart, the average female crude suicide rates in Asia between 2012 and 2014 were always lower than the average male crude suicide rates.
The difference between the female and male crude suicide rates from 2012 to 2014 ranges around 6.5 to 8 per 100k population.
Also, we can see that the sum of the average crude suicide rates of both genders are decreasing every year .
