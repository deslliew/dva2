Insights of Carbon Dioxide (CO2) Emissions in Asia (2017 - 2021)


High carbon emissions have significant consequences, affecting both the environment
and everyone within it. Therefore, it is imperative to closely
examine these emissions for the betterment of our environment
and the well-being of future generations.

In this webpage, we
will be looking at different visualizations that aim to provide
insights on the CO2 Emissions in Asia from 2017 - 2021.

Data Source: Global Fossil CO2 Emissions by Country (2002-2022)

CO2 Emission Severity in Asia (2017-2021)

It employs a captivating spectrum of blue colour luminance
to represent the severity of CO2 emissions across the continent.
The intensity of the blue colors directly correlates with the
magnitude of CO2 emissions in each country. Darker shades signify
higher emissions, while lighter tones indicate relatively lower rates.

From 2017 to 2021, it highlights the regions that have
made the most significant contributions to the planet's
CO2 emissions. Qatar and the United Arab Emirates contributes the
highest levels of CO2 emissions. On the flip side,
among the lighter blue luminance, you'll find
Nepal, Afghanistan, and Yemen, emblematic of countries
in Asia that have shown commendable restraint in CO2 emissions.



Assessing the Fluctuating Trends in Median CO2
Emissions per Capita in Asia (2017-2021)

Between the years 2017 and 2021, we can see the
fluctuating emissions, where the median hovers at an approximate
level of 3. Our chart reveals that these numbers underwent
a gradual ascent during the initial three years (2017-2019),
which might be due to an evolving landscape.

In the year 2020, a pivotal moment emerged as a global
pandemic unfolded, causing a minor dropped in emissions, reflecting
the substantial impacts of widespread lockdowns and lifestyle adjustments.

Average CO2 Emission Trends by Sector in Asia (2017-2021)

  • Construction and Building Materials Sector consistently had the lowest average CO2 emissions throughout the five years.

  • The CO2 emission rates in the Transportation and Oil & Gas sectors often fluctuated, with periods when one sector's emissions were higher than the other.

  • A collective drop in emissions across all sectors occurred in 2020 during the pandemic.
